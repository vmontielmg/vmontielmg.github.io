Introduction

The city of Hale'iwa, located on the island of Oahu's North Shore, is one of the island's most popular towns. The town of Hale'iwa is a laid-back surf town that offers visitors boutiques, surf shops, restaurants, food-stands, famous beaches, and much more.

I was fortunate enough to have visited Hale'iwa in 2014 freshly out of high school. I was staying in Waikiki for two weeks accompanied by my cousin and we decided to rent a car and visit the north shore for a day. We both fell in love with the town, food, and especially the atmosphere in Hale'iwa. It was a relaxed and warm town. We visited Matsumoto's, jumped off the rock at Waimea, and strolled through the town.

Attractions

  • Beaches

    • The island of Oahu has many beaches it is known for. Specifically the ones located in Hale'iwa. To name a few: Waimea Bay which is known for its "jump rock". Banzai Pipeline, popular for its big waves for surfing also bodyboarding. Shark's Cove for its snorkeling and not actual sharks. Sunset Beach also for snorkeling. All of these beaches are warm year-round in comparison to California's beaches.

  • Food

    • The town/city of Hale'iwa has a plethora of food stands and restaurants. One in particular which was my favorite place on the island was the famous Giovanni's Shrimp Truck. A graffiti-decorated truck that serves a dozen jumbo shrimp smothered in garlic, butter, lemon, and hot sauce with a side of white rice. Yea enough said.

    Hale'iwa Bowls is another popular food stand/shack located on Kamehameha Highway that serves acai bowls topped with fresh fruit as well as smoothies.

    Matsumoto Shave Ice is one of Oahu's most popular spots for shave ice. Established in 1951, Mastumoto's not only serves shave ice but as well sells souveniers, t-shirts, and other goods.
